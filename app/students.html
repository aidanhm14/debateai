How students use it

Four drills that beat passive review.

Rereading your notes doesn't prepare you for a hard question. Arguing does. These drills run you on a clock, against pushback, until the answer is instinct.

01

Interview prep

Job interviews, internship Supers, residency matches, grad-school panels. AI plays a thoughtful interviewer who probes behavioral stories, technical depth, and the resume line you're hoping they skim.

02

College admissions essays

Paste a draft. AI reads it like an adcom: looks for the specific moment, the real voice, the answer to "so what." Tells you which paragraph is generic in a way admissions readers will clock.

03

Thesis & oral defense

Load your argument. AI runs the hostile committee: the methods question you didn't plan for, the counter-example that breaks your claim, the "what would change your mind" probe you need a real answer to.

04

Socratic + seminar drills

Law school cold calls. Philosophy seminar. Business-case discussions. AI runs the Socratic sequence and flags where your reasoning flinches. Build the habit of thinking aloud under pressure.

The rooms you'll actually face

Practice the question before it's graded.

  • MBA + case interviewsConsulting casing, PE / finance fit interviews, behavioral STAR questions. AI runs the case and probes the math.
  • Med school / residency interviewsMMI stations, ethics probes, "tell me about a conflict" scenarios. Drill until your answers stop sounding rehearsed.
  • PhD qualifiers + dissertation defenseField-coverage questions, methodology stress tests, "explain this to someone outside your subfield" prompts.
  • Mooting / mock trialOral argument prep with bench questioning, witness examination drills, opening + closing rehearsal.
  • College admissions interviewsAlumni and adcom interviews. Practice the "why us" and "tell me about yourself" answers that aren't clichés.
  • Scholarship + fellowship applicationsRhodes, Marshall, Mitchell, Truman, Churchill. AI runs panel-style interviews where every answer sets up the next question.
