New: the one-screen live clash

Live voice debate against an AI opponent.

Speak your speech under the clock. The AI listens, interrupts, takes points of information, and gives you the next speech in real time. The model thinks in voice, not in transcripts, so the back-and-forth feels like a real round.

Five modes for different prep needs

  • Full round · every speech on compressed clocks, POIs mid-speech, a judge RFD (the written verdict) when the round ends. The flagship.
  • Cross-examination drill · pure cross-ex Q&A under timer; sharpen questions and concessions.
  • Opening speech · drill your PMC/1AC/first prop with an AI judge listening but not arguing back.
  • Rebuttal drill · the AI gives a speech; you respond. POIs not allowed; pure rebuttal craft.
  • Viva (oral exam) · academic defense mode; the AI examiner pushes on the weak link in your reasoning.

Sixteen distinct opponents

Each persona has its own voice and its own argumentative tendency. Eleanor argues from first principles. Marcus goes for the throat on warrants. Eight strengths and eight weaknesses per persona so you can scout before the round.

Format-aware, language-aware

The same engine knows how Asian Parli, WSDC, BP, APDA, PF, LD, Policy, Congress, MUN, and viva each differ on evidence rules, structure, and register. POIs in WSDC and BP. Framework first in LD. Tagged cards in Policy. No fabricated citations in impromptu formats. Voice rounds run in 14 languages including Hindi, Mandarin, Spanish, French, Arabic, and Bengali; the RFD comes back in the language you spoke.
