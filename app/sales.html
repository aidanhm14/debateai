How you'll use it

The reps your manager doesn't have time to run.

Role-play with an AI that won't get bored, won't go easy on you, and is available at 11pm the night before the renewal call.

01

Objection drills

Load your top 10 objections — "we're already using X," "budget's frozen," "let's circle back next quarter." AI fires them cold and scores your responses on relevance, confidence, and whether you actually moved toward the close.

02

Demo pitch rehearsal

Pitch against an AI playing your actual buyer persona — the budget they have, the incumbent they're comparing against, the pain they keep deflecting. Feedback on what landed, what drifted, what they tuned out.

03

Deal rescue + negotiation

Simulate hard conversations: procurement asking for 40% off, losing to an incumbent, a stalled renewal, a multi-stakeholder deal where the champion went quiet. Find the move you'd make on the call — before the call.

Role-play that sounds real

Every rep is a different buyer, not the same bot.

  • Skeptical CFOChallenges ROI math, wants payback inside the quarter, won't sign past a certain number.
  • Technical championLoves the product, can't convince their boss. Practice handing them the business case.
  • Procurement on the lineHas a playbook, wants concessions, is using your competitor as leverage. Drill the concession you can give versus the one that tanks your margin.
  • Incumbent defenderHappy enough with the current vendor. Practice displacing without badmouthing — the move that actually works.
  • Ghosting prospectWent silent after demo. Practice the bump-email cadence and the "breakup" message that gets a reply 30% of the time.
  • Renewal at riskUsage dropped, sentiment's off. Practice the save call — what you ask before you offer a discount.

Reps are how athletes get good. Same math for sales.

You can't close deals by watching Gong clips. Run the hard conversation out loud, find the weak turn, run it again until it lands. Start a rep and feel the difference.
