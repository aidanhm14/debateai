Case structure

Claim: the simple sentence the judge should remember.
Warrant: why the claim is true.
Impact: why the claim matters.
Weighing: why this impact beats the other side's best impact.

Debate case FAQ

How many arguments should I make?

Usually one or two excellent arguments beat four shallow ones. Depth makes rebuttal easier.

What makes a case persuasive?

Specific mechanisms, believable examples, and explicit comparison against the opponent's world.
