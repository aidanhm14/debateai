The philosophy

Most AI agrees with you. This one argues back.

A general assistant is built to help you. A debater is built to take the side you didn't prep — and beat you with it.

What makes it different

It takes the other side, and means it

A normal voice model is trained to agree, smooth things over, and hand back a tidy summary of both sides. A debater is built for the opposite instinct: find the weakest joint in your case and stand exactly where you're least ready for it. Helpfulness and opposition pull in different directions — most voice AI only has the first gear. This is built around the second.

It thinks in clash, not consensus

It won't list both sides and call that balance. It commits to a position and weighs it the way a judge does — magnitude, probability, timeframe — then defends it under pressure. When you push back, it doesn't fold to keep you comfortable. It tests whether your reasoning actually holds, because the point isn't to please you. The point is to find out if you're right.

It speaks the format, not just the language

A general model narrates. This one takes a point of information mid-sentence, cuts in when you overclaim, builds offense across six speeches, and writes a real ballot at the end. APDA, BP, WSDC, LD, PF, Policy, Congress — each is its own game with its own rules of engagement. It argues each one natively instead of flattening them into generic chat. That's the difference between an assistant that can talk about debate and an opponent that can do it.
