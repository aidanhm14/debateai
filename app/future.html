We train it to oppose you.

Built to keep you comfortable.

DebateIt

Built to find where you bend.

I think remote work obviously makes teams more productive.
"Obviously" is doing all the work. Productive at what, measured how, over what horizon? Drop the adverb and the case is thinner than you think. Defend it.
You leave with a case that survived contact.

Every general model is being tuned, this year, toward the same instinct: do not disagree with the user. That is the right call for an assistant and the exact wrong call for an opponent. We point the opposite instinct at the people who need the muscle most, and we hold the line when you go quiet.

04 — The future of proof
Writing used to prove you could think.
Now a machine writes it in eight seconds.

So the proof moved. It moved to the one place a model still cannot stand in for you: live, under pressure, in real time.

Anyone can generate
  • The cover letter
  • The admissions essay
  • The proposal
  • The polished take
Nobody can fake
  • A live defense
  • Reasoning in real time
  • Holding up under interruption
  • Changing a mind while it pushes back

That is what the credential measures. Not a debate badge. A verifiable signal of communication competence: responsiveness, clarity under interruption, listening accuracy, reasoning structure, how you handle disagreement. A cover letter could be you or the model. A coding test could be you or the model. A live oral defense at full intensity cannot fake itself.

Picture where that lands. Job applications. College admissions. Interviews. Remote hiring across borders. Leadership. Public speaking. Sales. Founder pitches. Every one of them is quietly moving back toward the spoken register, because writing has become evidence of nothing. The first credential that says this person can think and argue live is the one that cuts through.

05 — The flywheel

Every round makes the next one harder.

The model is a rental. Anyone can call the same six APIs. What no one else has is a labeled archive of which rebuttals beat which warrants, across ten formats, distilled every night and replayed into tomorrow's opponent.

  1. Humans debate the AI. Or humans debate humans, AI judging.
  2. Every round writes a row: motion, side, format, transcript, ballot.
  3. The best rounds get rated, by users and by hand.
  4. At 04:00 UTC a nightly pass distills the top rounds per format.
  5. That distillation is reinjected into tomorrow's opponent.
  6. The next debater trains against a sharper system, and makes it sharper still.

The labs train on "did the user click thumbs up." We train on did the rebuttal land. The first signal is everywhere on the internet. The second exists only inside live disagreement at adversarial intensity, and we are the ones in the room.
