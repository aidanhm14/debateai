The plan

Debate, run like a sport.

Two people argue live. An AI judge scores the round. A crowd backs the winner. That is the whole product. This page explains how it works and how it makes money, in plain words.

A round

Six minutes, start to ballot.

01

Pick a side

You get a topic, a format, and a clock. Government or opposition. No prep excuses.

02

Argue out loud

A real opponent or the AI pushes back in real time. It interrupts. It asks questions. It does not agree with you.

03

Read the ballot

The judge scores both sides and explains who won and why, seconds after you stop talking.

The brain

Most AI reads the internet. Our judge reads rounds.

Every debate here ends with a ballot: the arguments, the pushback, and a verdict. Those finished rounds become training examples, and the judge studies the best new ones every night.

That data barely exists anywhere else. The internet is full of essays and opinions, but almost nowhere do arguments come with a scored winner attached. Ours do. It is why the judge keeps getting harder to fool.

Rounds played → Judged debate data → Smarter judge → More debaters → Repeat
The business

Three ways money comes in.

STREAM 1

Players pay to train.

Serious practice costs a small subscription, like a gym membership. Schools and teams pay for rosters, brackets, and a season the coach can actually see, like a league fee.

STREAM 2

The crowd pays for the show.

Featured rounds draw an audience. Credit packs, sponsored matches, and ticketed events turn viewers into revenue, the way sports sell more than seats.

STREAM 3

The data pays twice.

First the rounds train our judge. Then, with names stripped out, they become something others pay for: schools verify scores, and AI labs license clean examples of arguments that won and lost.

Who paysWhat they get
A debaterFull practice tools, an AI opponent on demand, and a score that travels.
A school or teamRosters, brackets, and coach-visible progress across a season.
The crowdCredit packs and featured rounds worth showing up for.
Companies and AI labsVerified Communication Scores, judge access, and anonymized round data.

The rule underneath all three: rounds are anonymized before they train anything or leave the building, and students never touch real-money wagering. The product works without crossing either line.

Why this wins

A machine can write your essay. It cannot defend it for you.

Written work is easy to generate now, so schools and employers are moving back to live tests: interviews, oral defenses, questions you cannot paste. Speaking under pressure is the skill that still proves you understand. This is where people train it.
