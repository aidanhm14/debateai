The category line

General-purpose AI is optimized for cooperative dialogue. Debatable is optimized for adversarial cognition: live rounds against a clock, format-specific structure, rebuttals that pressure-test your logic, and a written judge ballot at the end. Same underlying models in some cases. Very different products.

What Debatable is built for

Format-accurate live debate sparring across 10 competitive formats: APDA, British Parliamentary, Asian Parliamentary, World Schools (WSDC), Public Forum, Lincoln-Douglas, Policy, Student Congress, Model UN, and Quick Clash. Six AI brains under the hood (Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, Open Lab). Voice rounds with a real clock, server-side voice activity detection so the AI can be interrupted, and a written reason-for-decision at the end of every round.

When a general LLM is the right pick

Research a topic from scratch. Draft a blog post. Summarize a long PDF. Brainstorm cases broadly. General assistants are excellent at all of these. They are not built to argue back, run a timed PMR, take a POI, or hand you a WUDC-scale judge ballot. That is a different surface.

Open question

The honest framing on every page in this section: pick the tool that matches the job. If you want a general writing or research assistant, ChatGPT and Claude are excellent. If you want to sit in the PM seat at 2am and grind a real round before a tournament, that is what Debatable is for.
