BYOK: bring your own Claude key

Debatable is the only mainstream debate platform that lets you bring your own Anthropic API key. Post-beta this is $1 per month, and on your key the round cap is removed; you pay Anthropic directly for the tokens. BYOK exists for power users who want cost transparency or a clean audit trail. It is intentionally Claude-only because the format-aware system prompt is tuned across brains differently and a mixed-key setup would silently break that tuning.

What Debatable adds on top of Claude

Every Claude call from inside Debatable gets four things prepended before the user prompt reaches the model:

  • Format voice block. Speech order, time limits, evidence rules, register, and known failure modes for the format you picked. Same block that the other five brains see, with light per-brain adjustments where the model needs them.
  • Exemplar past rounds. One to three admin-weighted past rounds matching the same motion and side, included as reference material so Claude has a concrete precedent for the shape of a good speech on this topic.
  • Learned patterns. A nightly distillation by Claude Haiku over the top-rated rounds in this format from the past 30 days, giving the model a current picture of what is winning on the circuit.
  • User style fingerprint. A periodically refreshed model of how you specifically argue, so the AI's response adjusts to the level and idiom you have actually been working at.

The voice round goes through OpenAI Realtime over WebRTC for the spoken interaction layer; the typed mode is where Claude is most often picked. Both share the same format-aware prompt stack and both write to the same generations log that feeds the learning loop.

When to use Claude directly instead

Reach for claude.ai directly when the job is not a round. Long-form research on a motion. Reading and summarizing a 60-page brief. Walking through a tricky philosophical framing. Drafting a tournament reflection. These are exactly what Claude is excellent at out of the box, and Debatable's format wrapping would just be friction.

Quick FAQ

Is Claude good for debate practice?

Claude is one of the strongest reasoning models available and one of the six brains Debatable runs under the hood. Used directly, it is excellent at drafting cases and walking through arguments. It does not enforce format-specific structure, run a timed voice round, or write a judge ballot in a circuit-judge register out of the box. Debatable wraps Claude with that infrastructure.

Can I bring my own Claude API key into Debatable?

Yes. BYOK is $1 per month post-beta. You pay Anthropic for the tokens, you get unlimited round count from Debatable. BYOK is Claude-only by design.

Why use Debatable when Claude is already strong on its own?

For debate practice specifically, you get the format voice block, the exemplar past rounds, the learned patterns, the voice round, and the judge mode on top of every Claude call. For non-debate tasks, use Claude directly; Debatable's format wrapping is the wrong tool.

Which Claude model does Debatable use?

Claude Opus 4.7 for the case generator and judge mode by default. Claude Haiku 4.5 for the nightly distillation pass. BYOK routes to the same defaults unless overridden.
