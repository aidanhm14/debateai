The reps gap

Every coach hits the same wall.

It is not talent and it is not effort. It is hours. There is one of you, a full roster, and a season that does not wait. Reps are the thing you cannot manufacture, so they go to the few kids who already get the most.

Novices

need structure

A first-year needs a hundred low-stakes rounds to stop freezing. You do not have a hundred volunteers to run them against.

Varsity

need pressure

Your top kids have outgrown the room. They need an opponent who interrupts, takes POIs, and goes after the weak line. Most weeks that opponent is you, once.

Everyone

needs faster feedback

A ballot a week later teaches less than a ballot at the buzzer. You cannot write forty of them by hand, so most rounds go unjudged.

DebateIt does not replace you. It runs the rounds you cannot be in, so your hours go to judgment, strategy, and the kid who actually needs you instead of refereeing drills.

Rung four · read the round

A flowing drill the whole room does at once.

Ask a room who won a round and half of them shrug. Give them a call to make and they watch differently: which warrant landed, which rebuttal was a dodge, who won the clash and not just the volume. Then they see the ballot and find out if their read was right.

That is what The Floor is for. It runs on play-money, the AI settles the round, and it trains the exact judgment you want every flow to show. Engagement and adjudication practice, not a casino.

Where you can stop reading and trust it

For students, this is play-money. The Floor uses play credits, no card, nothing to win or lose. It is a way to keep score on how well a debater reads a round, the same instinct you grade on a flow.

Any real-stakes layer comes later, only where it is legal, and only for verified adults. Minors never touch redeemable cash, today or in any future version. The point is sharper judging and a louder audience for your program, full stop.

Straight answers

The questions you are already asking.

Will this teach my kids bad habits?
It is format-specific by design. Policy gets tagged cards, LD gets value and criterion, BP gets extensions and whip, parli gets impromptu with no fake citations. It does not flatten every event into generic debate English, which is exactly the failure mode of a general chatbot.
Will it replace what I do?
No, and it is not trying to. It runs reps so kids come back to you further along. Your time moves up the value chain, to strategy, judgment, and the conversations only a coach can have.
Is the prediction layer gambling?
For students, no. The Floor is play-money, full stop. Any future redeemable layer is adults-only, legal-market-only, and walled off from minors and competitors. What your team uses is a judging drill, not a bet.
Who built it?
A national APDA champion. Format accuracy is treated as a product requirement, not a prompt trick, because the person who built it actually argued at the top of the circuit and knows where generic AI gets debate wrong.
