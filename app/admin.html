How to read Mission Control

Everything you need to drive the dashboard in one screen.

1 The time window

The bar at the top has four spans: 24h, 3d, 7d, 30d. Pick one and every time-windowed panel re-pulls for exactly that span. Your choice is remembered on this browser, so a reload keeps it.

2 What moves with the window

These panels all read the window you pick:

  • Hour · day heatmap
  • Format · brain mix
  • Devices
  • Browsers
  • Top pages
  • Power users
  • Round-complete funnel
  • Founder video

The heatmap, mix, devices, browsers and top pages all come from one query, so they always agree on the span.

3 What stays put

A few panels sit on a different axis and ignore the window on purpose:

  • KPI strip (top row) is lifetime totals. "+N last 7d" on Total users is a fixed 7-day delta.
  • Activity chart switches grain with its own daily / weekly / monthly tabs, not the window.
  • Live activity always shows the latest 60 events, streaming.
  • Weekly cohorts are measured in weeks. use its 8w / 12w tabs.

4 Reading the key panels

  • Round-complete funnel: starts vs completes, plus the same rate weighted by unique users so a few power users can't inflate it.
  • Founder video: play → 25 → 50 → 75 → complete drop-off. retention% is each bucket over plays.
  • Heatmap: darker red = a busier UTC hour. good for spotting when your users actually show up.
  • Power users: ranked by engagement score in the window. click a row for that user's full session inspector.

5 Freshness

The dashboard is a snapshot, not a live stream. Auto-refresh is off by default to save Firestore reads. hit ↻ Refresh for a fresh pull, or flip Auto-refresh on to poll every 5 minutes. The live activity tail updates on its own.
