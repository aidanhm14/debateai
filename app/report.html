01 · Product

What this is.

One paragraph on the product, one block on what it isn't.

Debatable is the chess.com of debate. Two surfaces, one engine. On the human side: live human-vs-human rounds, online tournaments, and public rankings, all judged by the same AI ballot. On the AI side: a voice-first sparring partner that argues against you, takes points of information, runs the clock, and writes you a judge's ballot when the round ends.

The product is voice. The chat box exists for fallback. The moat is that the AI, both as opponent and as judge, sounds format-correct: an APDA round sounds nothing like a Policy round, a WSDC bench sounds nothing like a Congress speech. That is the part every other AI debate tool has skipped, and the part that matters to anyone who does this for sport.

Is

A tournament + ranking platform for human-vs-human debate. An adversarial AI sparring partner. A judge that hands you a written RFD on either. A learning loop that compounds across rounds.

Isn't

A chatbot. A grammar tool. A writing assistant. A research engine. Not built to agree with you; built to compete.

02 · Wedge

Why this exists.

Five things, ranked by how hard they are to copy. The first one is the moat.

1 · Format accuracy

Policy gets tagged-card delivery and evidence rules. LD gets value and criterion. BP gets extensions and whip structure. APDA stays impromptu with no fabricated citations. WSDC gets reply speeches; Congress gets gavel-conscious decorum; MUN gets diplomatic register. Every other AI debate tool speaks generic "Harvard debate society" English. This requires actually knowing the formats, not just wrapping GPT.

2 · Voice round + judge RFD

Sub-200ms turn-taking. You can interrupt, take points of information, run out of time, get cut off. At the end of the round you get a written ballot with magnitude, probability, and timeframe weighing. That is the moat against ChatGPT: voice plus timer plus format-correct ballot.

3 · Built by a national champion

Most AI-product founders can say "I used AI to build this." Very few can say "I actually argued in this format at the top of the country." The voice prompts are extracted from real APDA notes, real cross-x patterns, real ballots.

4 · Six brains, real voice

Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, Open Lab. Streaming TTS on the paid tier through ElevenLabs, Inworld, and Cartesia, with OpenAI gpt-4o-mini-tts as the per-persona-steered free tier. BYOK lets power users bring their own Anthropic key for a dollar a month.

5 · A learning loop that gets sharper every night

Every typed AI turn and every voice-round transcript writes to Firestore. The next time a user picks the same motion in the same format, the system pulls the highest-rated past rounds into the prompt as reference exemplars. A nightly Haiku pass distills the top rounds per format into a "patterns that work" block injected on every subsequent generation. The AI on a given motion today is meaningfully different from the AI on that motion last month. (Section 5 explains the architecture.)

04 · Surfaces

What you actually do in it.

Every surface in the app, what it does, and where it lives. Short on time: the first three are the ones to know.

Voice debate /voice-debate
Live WebRTC round through OpenAI's Realtime API. Sub-200ms turns. Server-side voice activity detection means the AI cuts in when you slow down, the way a real opponent would. Five modes: full round, cross-examination only, POI drill, opening only, closing only. Five-mode selector + post-round RFD generated by the same engine. Six rounds per hour per IP, App Check gated.
Format library /debate-it
Eleven formats with full speech-by-speech timings: Quick Clash, APDA, BP, Asian Parli, Worlds (WUDC), LD, PF, Policy, Congress, Viva, MUN. Each format carries its own evidence rules (Policy uses tagged cards; APDA forbids fabricated citations), structure, register, and timing. Selecting a format swaps the AI's system prompt to the format-specific voice block.
Sixteen AI personas across the app
Sixteen voice slots drive the app's debaters; in the voice round they surface as named characters with their own voices and tendencies: Cassidy, Marcus, Priya, Eleanor, Rosa, Aisha, Maya, and Theo, plus Dr. Iyer, an Indian-English measured academic register for vivas (default in the Counter extension). Each has its own voice (ElevenLabs / Inworld / Cartesia on Pro; OpenAI gpt-4o-mini-tts with per-persona instructions on Free) and its own argumentative tendencies in the system prompt.
Six AI brains across the app
Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, Open Lab, each routed through its own Netlify function (claude.mjs, openai-chat.mjs, gemini.mjs, grok.mjs, deepseek.mjs, openlab.mjs). User can pick the brain or let the app default. Free tier runs the cheaper sibling models; Pro switches to the flagship. DeepSeek goes direct to the DeepSeek API; Open Lab routes through OpenRouter and defaults to Nous Hermes 4 405B. BYOK is Anthropic-only, labeled accordingly.
Judge RFD any round
After any round (voice or typed) the same engine writes you a debate-judge-style ballot. Magnitude, probability, timeframe. Specific drops cited. What to fix next. The ballot is the retention moment.
Live human rounds + tournaments /live, /live-round
The chess.com surface. Daily.co video rooms for human-vs-human debate, judged by the same AI ballot engine. Schedule-or-go-now flow, auto-mic toggle on the speaker panel, AI judge generates the RFD when the round ends. Online tournaments and bracket play stack on top of the same room infrastructure. Solves the "I have no one to spar with at 11pm before a tournament" problem, then escalates into "I can run a full tournament from this app."
Topics Hub + Motions
Rolling topic bank for the four formats that use one: PF, LD, Policy, Congress. APDA is impromptu so it routes to the random motion generator instead. Updated against current event cycles.
Disclosures /disclosures
Opencaselist-style community case board. Three privacy lanes (Private, Team, Public). Users publish their cases for any signed-in debater to read or import; vote up the strong ones. Quality floor on publish (length, profanity, link-spam). Content compounds without us writing copy.
Counter chrome extension /extension
A separate front door for the same engine, branded "Counter: Oral Exam Trainer (by Debatable)." Students highlight a passage on any page, hit Connect, and defend it out loud against Dr. Iyer. Built for the viva use case across CBSE/ICSE board exams, IIT/NEET interviews, and dissertation defense; loads voice-debate.html?ext=1&mode=counter in the side panel.
Global ranking /leaderboard
The chess.com Elo equivalent. Public ladder across every format, consent-gated opt-in (writers can flip only their own UID). AI-judged rounds and human-vs-human rounds feed the same rating. A debater can climb the ladder against the AI at 2am, then defend their position against a human on the live surface the next day. Plus a 52-term vocab quiz spanning debate flow, Policy theory, philosophy, and elevated vocabulary; /learn indexes the format guides and case-prep walkthroughs.
Profile dashboard /profile
Per-user XP, streak, format-mastery breakdown, achievements, and recent rounds, all judged on the same AI ballot the leaderboard uses. The retention surface; gives a returning user a reason to come back the next day rather than the next week.
Geo landings /india, /us
Audience-tuned entry pages for the two largest segments. /india leads with Asian Parli, WSDC, BP and India-coded palette. /us leads with PF, LD, Policy, Worlds, Congress and tournament names (TOC, Glenbrooks, Harvard, Berkeley, Greenhill, Emory). Same engine, different door.
Per-user admin /admin
Pull any user's full activity timeline: sessions, page views, generations, conversions, every gtag event mirrored through the per-user app_event log. Profile header, KPI strip, event-mix table, 200-event scrollback. Support and debugging links resolve directly with ?uid=.
05 · Learning Loop

The system gets sharper every night.

The part a one-glance demo misses. Three layers, all shipped, all in production as of 2026-05-13.

Layer 1 · Capture every turn

Every typed AI turn (case, opp_attack, rebuttal, judge, debate-chat, casual, vision, resolution) writes the full prompt-to-output triple to the generations Firestore collection. Voice rounds flush the entire turn-by-turn transcript to /api/log-generation on session end. Signed-in usage only; anon users can't pollute the corpus.

Layer 2 · Reference rounds injected at runtime

When a user starts a round, the server pulls 1–3 admin-weighted past rounds matching the same motion + format + side and prepends them as REFERENCE ROUNDS to the system prompt. The four AI-brain functions all read this before applying voice guidelines. A 1-hour in-memory cache keeps per-request Firestore reads near zero.

Layer 3 · Nightly distillation

Every night at 04:00 UTC, a scheduled function runs Claude Haiku over the previous 30 days of top-rated generations per format (rating ≥ 4 or saved). It extracts a "patterns that work" block per format and writes it to learning_distillations/{format}. Every subsequent generation reads that doc and appends a LEARNED PATTERNS block to the system prompt.

Final system-prompt order at runtime: [reference rounds] + base system + [learned patterns] + [voice guidelines]. Voice rules come last so they always win conflicts.

"This is the only way an AI debate product compounds. Generic LLMs forget every round. Ours remembers what won."
06 · Architecture

How it's built.

For technical readers; skip if you trust that it works. One repo, Netlify edge, four LLM providers, four TTS providers, Firebase for state.

Single-repo React via CDN Netlify Functions Claude · GPT · Gemini · Grok ElevenLabs · Inworld · Cartesia · OpenAI TTS OpenAI Realtime · WebRTC Firebase Auth + Firestore Stripe + Razorpay PWA · service worker

Front end

One large single-file React app (app/debate-it.html) using React via CDN with a el(tag, props, ...children) alias for React.createElement. No JSX, no build step, intentional. The landing page, voice-debate page, live rooms, and extension iframe are sibling HTML pages sharing the same CSS tokens.

Voice path

For voice, the server mints an ephemeral OpenAI Realtime client secret (POST /v1/realtime/client_secrets), the browser exchanges WebRTC SDP directly with OpenAI (POST /v1/realtime/calls), and session config is pushed over the data channel. The server is never in the audio path, so latency is OpenAI-direct.

Text path

Each LLM provider has its own Netlify function (claude.mjs, openai-chat.mjs, gemini.mjs, grok.mjs, deepseek.mjs, openlab.mjs) that prepends the format-specific voice block from lib/voice-guidelines.mjs (server-side so view-source can't scrape it), then layers reference rounds and learned-patterns blocks per Section 5.

TTS

The tts.mjs function routes by tier: ElevenLabs eleven_turbo_v2_5 as Pro default, Inworld inworld-tts-1.5-max and Cartesia sonic-2 as Pro opt-ins, OpenAI gpt-4o-mini-tts with per-persona instructions for Free and fallback. Intensity, premium flag, and persona key flow from the client.

Data + payments

Firebase Auth (Google only), Firestore for user state, App Check gated on the sensitive endpoints, rate-limited 6 rounds/hr/IP on Realtime. Payments via Stripe, with Razorpay landing for India before the next paid push.

08 · Traction

Where we are, honestly.

The acquisition curve is the headline. The funnel is the problem. Both are diagnosed and the fixes are shipping. Numbers are from Firebase Analytics through 2026-04-19; the next traction refresh is due.

Acquisition

  • Early beta. Live numbers are small and honest; see /community for the rolling 30-day count and the public sign-up history. The room is still being built; the product matters more than the headline.
  • Geographic mix is global from day one: India is the strongest signal (English-first, debate-heavy education plus a viral cross-border ad channel), then the US, Philippines, and Brazil, with a long tail of countries holding at least one returning user.
  • Primary acquisition channel: Reddit, then paid social, then organic search (currently near zero, see Roadmap).

Retention

  • D7 cohort retention: 3.5%. Small but real.
  • D8: 6.4%, up from 2.1% the prior week.
  • Among users who actually signed in (not the anon population), DAU/MAU runs 18–40%. The product retains once it gets past its own gate.

The funnel

Two known leaks, both diagnosed, neither hidden.

  • Sign-in drop (62%): caused by iOS Safari ITP, in-app browsers (Instagram, FB, TikTok), and strict ad blockers preventing Firebase from loading. Fixed by reversing the full-screen pricing gate to advise-only and detecting in-app browsers in-flow. Sign-in is now an upgrade, not a blocker.
  • Round-complete drop (was 88%, now lower): caused by a 30-second TTS warm-up countdown in a UX experiment that broke under slow Indian mobile latency. Patched 2026-05-06: when first audio chunks land and ≥2 are queued, the countdown is bypassed and playback fires immediately. Slow-network case unchanged.

Revenue

MRR is $0 as of writing. Stripe is wired but the conversion-tracking allowlist was broken in three layers; all three patched this week, plus the customer.subscription.created webhook for billing-portal upgrades. Razorpay lands before the next paid push so India can convert in INR.

Spend to date

~$1K total, ads + infrastructure combined. Solo-built, no payroll, no co-founder.

09 · Roadmap

Where this goes.

Six-month view. Order matters. The first thing is the gate to everything else.

Now → Month 2 · Monetize the existing base

Razorpay for India. First paid conversions. Validate the $1 BYOK and $10 Individual tiers in the 80% of traffic that's already here. Floor MRR target before opening a pre-seed.

Month 1 → Month 4 · Tournaments + Elo (the chess.com build-out)

Online tournaments on top of the existing /live room infrastructure: open registration, format-specific brackets, swiss + single-elim, AI-judged elimination rounds with optional human-judge override on finals. A unified Elo across AI-judged solo rounds and human-vs-human rounds: one ladder, one rating. First public invitational seeded from the existing user base; recurring weekly opens after. This is the surface that turns a sparring tool into a sport.

Month 2 → Month 6 · US Pro tier + schools beachhead

LinkedIn outbound to 1L/2L law students for the $50/year Team tier (US conversion multiplier). "Coach Pro Forever" free for any verified debate coach, no questions asked, gating the per-seat school sale. Pilot programs with the first 3 US Urban Debate Leagues and the first 18 UWC schools. Schools get a private bracket inside the tournament engine for intramural play.

Month 6 → Month 12 · Native mobile

Capacitor wrap of the existing PWA. Six-week MVP. Structurally fixes the in-app-browser auth bug and unlocks push notifications, which is the missing retention lever.

SEO + content engine, in parallel

Organic search is currently near zero because the splash interstitial at root was indexing an empty page. Root now serves the full landing again. SoftwareApplication + FAQPage structured data is in. Search Console verification placeholder is wired; once verified, the queries this site already ranks for become visible. Distinct from this: a TikTok + Instagram content engine (5 pillars, 30 posts in 30 days) is the consumer-acquisition lever next to paid.

The ESL line

Core TAM is ~575,000 competitive debaters globally. Adjacent (forensics, MUN, mock trial, law students) is ~8M. The stretch is the 1.5 billion people learning English. Speak hit $20M ARR at ~2M MAU on the AI-English-tutor thesis; Duolingo did 100M MAU at $84/yr on grammar. "Practice speaking English by arguing with an AI" is the same thesis applied to argumentation. If the data supports it, the debate market becomes a rounding error.
