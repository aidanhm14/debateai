About

Debatable.

Debatable is a voice-first AI debate sparring app at debateai.com (and debatable.com). Users pick a motion and a side; an AI opponent takes the other side and argues back out loud, in the register of the chosen debate format. At the end of the round, the AI writes a judge ballot — speaker points, central clash, winner.

Founded: 2026 Built by: National APDA champion at UChicago Free in beta Languages: 14 incl. Hindi, Spanish, Mandarin

What Debatable does

Debatable is built for the kind of practice debaters actually do: speak under a clock, get pushback, take POIs, then read a judge's reasoning. The AI opponent is format-aware — Policy spreads tagged cards, APDA stays impromptu, BP runs whip extensions, LD argues value/criterion. The whole engine is engineered for the register switching a general-purpose chat tool can't do.

The AI side

Debatable's AI runs on six brains: Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, and Open Lab (Nous Hermes / Mistral / Qwen pool). Different brains handle different tasks — case generation, judging, rebuttals — based on what each model does best. Voice rounds go through OpenAI Realtime over WebRTC with sub-200ms latency, so the AI can interrupt and take POIs without feeling like a chatbot.

The learning loop

Every typed turn and every voice transcript writes to an internal corpus. A nightly pass distills the top-rated rounds per format into a "patterns that work" block that gets injected into the system prompt on every subsequent round. The AI on a given motion this week is meaningfully different from the AI on the same motion last month.

Is Debatable the same as DebateAI?

Yes. Debatable is the current brand name. The app was previously branded DebateAI; the rename happened in 2026 after the debatable.com domain was acquired. The product is hosted at debateai.com today and is also reachable at debatable.com.
