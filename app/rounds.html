How async rounds work

Nobody has to be online at the same time.

Every other way to debate here needs two people free at the same hour. Across a circuit that spans every timezone, that hour usually does not exist. An async round removes it.

Three turns, traded like letters.

You pick a motion and open for Prop in 90 seconds. Someone takes the Opposition in 120. You close in 60. Video or audio, recorded straight in the browser, no scheduling and no room to join.

A real judge at the end, not a thumbs up.

Every turn gets transcribed, then the AI writes a format specific ballot: winner, speaker points, and a reason for decision that argues with the actual flow. Eight formats, from Quick Clash to Policy.

It is the only surface here that survives a thin queue.

Spar needs someone waiting right now. Live needs a room. This needs one person, today, and a second person whenever they get to it.

  • Anyone can answer. Every public challenge on the board is open to whoever gets there first. No invitation, no partner, no arranged time.
  • Anyone can watch. Finished rounds stay up with both speeches and a transcript of every turn, so a round is something to learn from, not just something you were in.
  • Anyone can vote. The AI writes the ballot. Everyone else votes on whether it got the round right.
