The AI debate coach, explained

An AI debate coach you can talk to out loud, any hour. It runs targeted drills, hears your speeches against a clock, and tells you exactly what to fix. The more rounds you run, the better it reads your style. The coach is part of DebateIt, a voice-first debate training platform with 15 formats, 6 AI brains, and a live sparring board.

Available drills

The coach runs seven drill types, each targeting a different competitive skill: POI gauntlet (composure under interruption), rebuttal sprint (one-breath refutations), impact weighing (proving your harm outweighs), framework clash (defending your case philosophy), cross-examination (pinning opponents with rapid questions), steelman then break (building their best case, then taking it apart), and open spar (free-form practice on any motion).

The coach follows you across DebateIt

A floating coach orb sits in the corner on every page. Click it to jump into a drill from wherever you are: the prep room, the live sparring board, or the fundamentals guide. Finish a drill, close the session, and return to what you were doing. The coach remembers your last fourteen days of rounds and applies that context to every drill.

What is an AI debate coach?

A voice partner that runs targeted drills, hears your speeches against a clock, and gives instant feedback on structure, warrants, weighing, and delivery. DebateIt's coach loads your debater profile and recent rounds, so it knows your format and the moves you lean on.

How is an AI debate coach different from a human coach?

A human coach is limited by their schedule. The AI debate coach is available any hour, runs unlimited reps, and tracks every round you have done. Use it for volume reps between sessions, not instead of a human.

Is the AI debate coach free?

Yes. DebateIt is free in beta, the coach included. Sign in with Google to save your rounds and let the coach learn your style over time.

Which debate formats does the coach support?

Public Forum, Lincoln-Douglas, Policy, British Parliamentary, APDA, Asian Parliamentary, World Schools, Congress, and MUN, plus career formats like mock trial, negotiation, and pitch defense. New to the formats? Start with the debate fundamentals guide.
