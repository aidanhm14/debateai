FAQ

What's the difference between matchmaker and random?

Matchmaker is the queue plus the waitlist marketplace. You can either wait for a partner in 30-60s or browse open invites and DM someone to pick a time. Random is the one-click stranger draw — no waitlist, no DM, just pick a prompt and hit Find.

Do I need to sign in to debate live?

Anonymous visitors can try the AI rounds at /debate-ai and /voice-debate. Human-vs-human live modes require a Google sign-in as an anti-bot measure — sign-in unlocks all three modes (matchmaker, random, scheduled) with the same 10-round Free tier.

What formats do live rounds support?

Asian Parliamentary, WSDC, BP, APDA, PF, LD, Policy, Congress, MUN, and Quick Clash. Scheduled mode locks the format up front (each format has its own speech timing and judging rules). Matchmaker and random default to Quick Clash for speed; you can switch formats in the room.

Is there a tournament I can enter?

A flagship online tournament with AI prelims and human elims is on the year-out roadmap. Today, /live/scheduled hosts individual rounds with AI judging — the closest thing to a tournament round you can run on your own schedule.

How is this different from voice rounds against an AI?

Voice rounds against an AI live at /voice-debate — useful for solo practice, 24/7, with format-accurate AI opponents. Live debates here are human-vs-human: real people on the other side, real interruptions, real disagreement. The AI only judges at the end.

Don't want a live round right now?

Spar against the AI any time of day — voice or typed, format-accurate, judged at the end. No queue, no scheduling, no waiting on the room to fill.
