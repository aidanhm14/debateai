How it works

A real round, end to end. From posting the motion to reading the judge's verdict: what you click, what you see, and what each side has to do.

1
~30 sec

Continue with Google

Both speakers need a Google account. Once.

Details
  • Click Sign in top-right, or you'll be prompted.
  • Your name + photo label your speeches. Your email syncs your record across devices.
  • No password. Google handles login. Used only to log you in. I never email you.
2
~1 min

Schedule or join a debate

Two paths in: post your own, or accept someone else's.

Details
  • Posting: motion + side + format + when (Now / +30m / scheduled). Choose reveal, full motion, topic only, or theme only, to control how much the public board sees before kickoff.
  • Accepting: click any open card, confirmation modal explains what you're committing to, enter contact info + opt-in.
  • Already have a friend lined up? Click Schedule a human debate, pick a time, and send them the post.
3
instant

Calendar invite + room link

Both sides get the same kickoff time and the same in-page video room.

Details
  • One click adds the round to Google Calendar with the room link in the description.
  • Or download the .ics for Apple Calendar / Outlook.
  • The video room is created on first arrival. No install, no separate account.
4
at kickoff

Show up, embedded video

Click Start judged round on the accepted card. Both sides land on the same page.

Details
  • Video room embeds in the page: call on the left, speech panel on the right.
  • Allow camera + mic when the browser asks. Test by saying hi before the timer starts.
  • Decide who's speaking first per the format spec (it's shown at the top of the panel).
5
format length

Run the round, speech by speech

Format-aware timer enforces the real speech sequence. Both sides see the same state.

Details
  • Active speaker hits ▶ Start speech when ready. The clock doesn't auto-start, so prep time is yours.
  • Mic auto-transcribes (Chrome / Safari). Space = pause/resume, M = mic, Enter = end speech.
  • Cross-ex / POIs: just keep talking. End the speech when the format clock is done.
  • Whoever is speaking does the typing/mic. The other side watches and preps their response.
6
~30 sec

AI ballot, both sides see it

After the last speech, the AI judges. Tournament-grade RFD, on both screens.

Details
  • Winner + speaker points scaled to the format (28+ in APDA = strong, 27-30 in PF, etc.).
  • 3-6 sentence RFD naming the deciding clash and why one side won it.
  • Per-speaker feedback: what worked + one specific thing to fix.
  • Saved to both speakers' records. Copy or rematch in one click.
What you need: laptop with cam + mic Time: 10 min (Quick Clash) to 90 min (Policy) Browser: Chrome / Safari for live mic transcription Cost: free
