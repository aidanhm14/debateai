How founders use it

Three reps that keep you out of dilution.

The argument you can't answer in the meeting is the argument that re-prices your round. Find it first.

01

VC pitch rehearsal

Load your deck. AI plays a seed, Series A, or late-stage partner — different partner personas probe different things. Get hit with the "what's your moat actually" and the "how is this not a feature" before the real meeting.

02

Board + investor updates

Rehearse the monthly update with an AI playing your most active investor. Practice answering the question you've been dodging in the footnotes. Know which miss to lead with and which context to frame around it.

03

Hard conversation drills

Co-founder breakup. Firing a key hire. Telling the team runway's short. AI plays the other side — honest, emotional, sometimes hostile. Find the phrasing that's direct without torching the relationship.

Who shows up on the other side

Every rep is a different character, not a generic chatbot.

  • Numbers-first partnerAudits your CAC/LTV in real time. Will derail on a suspicious unit-economics claim. Practice defending the metric you're soft on.
  • Thesis-driven partnerWants to know why you, why now. Tests market timing logic. Practice the "why this isn't just a feature Shopify will ship" answer.
  • Skeptical board memberHas seen this movie before. Asks about the competitor you didn't mention. Knows the hire you overpromised last quarter.
  • Potential co-founderConsidering joining. Asks about equity split, decision rights, what happens when you disagree. Practice the honest answers.
  • Key hire you're trying to closeHas competing offers. Wants to know why your vision beats the bigger check. Practice the close that doesn't over-promise.
  • Angry customer about to churnEnterprise ACV on the line. Practice the save call — what to offer, what to never offer, how to reframe the relationship.

The rep beats the deck.

You can iterate a deck forever. You can't iterate in the meeting. Run the hard rep out loud, find the weak turn, run it again. Show up in the real room already having heard the hard question.
