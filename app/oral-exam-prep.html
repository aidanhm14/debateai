Why this works

An oral exam is a two-sided question in disguise.

You walk in holding a position: your thesis, your method, your answer. The examiner's job is to take the other side and see if your side survives. That is exactly what debate is. Evaluating both sides of a claim, then defending one of them under fire.

Your side
"My results show X."
"I chose this method because Y."
"The argument in chapter three holds."
vs
The examiner's side
"Couldn't your data also mean the opposite?"
"Why not the simpler method everyone else used?"
"Walk me through the strongest objection to chapter three."

A debater trains to know the other side better than the people on it. That is why the same engine that argues against a national-circuit debater is the right one to examine a student. It already knows where the weak link is, because finding it is the whole job.

What you get

Pushback, not praise.

A chatbot tells you the work is great. An examiner finds the crack and presses on it. This is built to do the second thing, because that is what the real room does.

1
One question at a time.

Real examiners ask one precise question and let you think. The AI does the same. No pile-on, no five-at-once, no rambling. You answer, it listens, then it goes after the weakest part of what you said.

2
It takes the other side.

For every claim you make, the AI holds the strongest version of the counter. Not a strawman. The objection your actual panel would raise, so the first time you hear it is in practice, not in the exam.

3
A verdict you can act on.

It closes with what landed, where you hesitated, and the one answer to tighten before the real thing. Examiner-voice feedback, the kind your teacher would recognize.

The flow

Five seconds in. Two minutes to a real defense.

01
Name your topic

Paste your thesis, your project summary, or just name the subject you are being examined on. One line is enough to set the scene.

02
Connect the mic

One tap unlocks voice. The AI opens the way a real examiner does: "Tell me what you set out to show, and why it matters."

03
Defend out loud

Three to five probes. You speak. It hears your answer, then takes the opposing side of your weakest claim and asks again.

04
Get the verdict

What was sharp, what wobbled, one thing to fix. Run it again until the soft spot stops being soft.

Common questions

Before you start.

How is this different from asking ChatGPT to quiz me?

A general assistant defaults to being agreeable and asks scattershot questions. This is configured as an examiner. It asks one question at a time, takes the strongest opposing side of your claim, and is voice-first, so the drill is answering under pressure rather than reading. That voice round is the part a real viva tests and a text chat can't rehearse.

Why does a debate company make an oral exam tool?

Because they are the same skill. A debater spends every round evaluating both sides of a question and then defending one. An oral exam puts you on one side and sends an examiner at the other. The engine that knows how to find the strongest objection to a debate case is the one that knows how to cross-examine a student. See the main app →

Do I have to speak, or can I type?

Speaking is the point, and the voice round is the default. A typed fallback exists if you don't have a mic, but the thing a viva actually tests is whether you can hold an answer out loud while someone pushes, so use voice if you can.
