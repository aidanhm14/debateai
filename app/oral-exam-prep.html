The loop

What actually happens in a viva?

Strip away the nerves and it is one loop, run five or six times. Read it once and you will see what it is.

1
You make a claim.
"This method was the right call." "My results show X."
2
The examiner challenges it.
"Why not the simpler method everyone else used?"
3
You defend it.
Reasons, out loud, on the spot. No second draft.
4
The examiner changes angles.
"Fine. Now what breaks if your main assumption is wrong?"
5
You adapt.
Hold the line, or concede the right amount and keep moving.

That is a debate. One side defends, the other probes for the crack. DebateIt has run that loop across millions of rounds. The oral exam room just points the examiner at you.

A real round

How a round actually runs.

Five steps, about two minutes. You name what you are defending, then the examiner does the rest.

Motion
"Explain your thesis, and why it matters." You open.
Question
"Why should I believe that?"
Challenge
"What if your core assumption is wrong?"
Follow-up
"Then how does your argument change?"
Evaluation
Clarity. Reasoning. Responsiveness. And the one answer to tighten before the real thing.

The ballot

The examiner scores five things.

Not whether you got the "right answer." Whether you can defend your reasoning when it is questioned. The same five DebateIt measures everywhere.

Clarity
Could a smart person follow your point the first time?
Reasoning
Does the claim actually stand on the reasons you gave?
Responsiveness
Did you answer the question asked, or the one you wanted?
Listening
Did you hear the real objection before you replied?
Structure
Did the answer have a spine, or did it wander?

The same five a DebateIt judge scores in a debate round, and the same five that land on your DebateIt credential. One skill, measured one way, wherever in the building you practice it.

Common questions

Before you start.

Why is the oral exam room part of a debate platform?

Because a viva is a debate. You hold a position, the examiner takes the other side and probes for the crack, and you adapt. It is the same skill, so it runs on the same engine. The part of DebateIt that knows how to find the strongest objection to a debate case is the part that knows how to cross-examine a student. See the main app →

Do I have to speak, or can I type?

Speaking is the point, and the voice round is the default. A typed fallback exists if you have no mic, but the thing a viva tests is whether you can hold an answer out loud while someone pushes. Use voice if you can.

Will it work for my subject?

Yes. Name the topic and paste a line or two of what you are defending. It examines science practicals, humanities theses, social-science methods, law and policy positions, and admissions essays the same way: hold your claim, take the other side, press.
