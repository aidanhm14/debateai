The Problem

Debate prep hasn't changed in 20 years

Most debaters still prep the same way: Google around for 45 minutes, write a mediocre case on the bus to the tournament, and hope they don't get a motion they've never thought about. There's a better way.

  • Feedback is rare

    You get judge feedback maybe 4 times a semester. That's not a feedback loop — that's a feedback trickle.

  • Practice partners are hard to find

    Your partner is busy. The team meeting is Thursday. The tournament is Saturday. Good luck.

  • Philosophy stays surface-level

    Everyone cites Rawls. Almost nobody knows the difference between early and late Rawls, or when each one actually helps your case.

  • No way to drill weak spots

    You know your MG extensions are weak, but there's no tool to practice just that speech against realistic opposition.

Features

Six tools, one engine

Every tool is designed around a specific bottleneck in debate prep. None of them exist to look impressive on a features page. They exist because competitive debaters need them.

Case Generator

Enter any motion. Get a structured case with a framework, three interlocking arguments, warrant chains, a compound impact, and pre-built A2 blocks for the five most likely opposition responses. Choose between quick prep (tournament speed) or deep prep (practice depth).

Why it works: You learn case architecture by seeing hundreds of well-built cases. After generating 20, you start pattern-matching instinctively — the same skill top debaters build over years of tournaments.

Round Simulator

Practice every speech type against an AI opponent that responds to your actual arguments — not canned lines.

Why it works: You can't learn to box by hitting a bag. You need a sparring partner who hits back.

Philosophy Engine

50+ philosophers with period-accurate depth. Know when to cite early vs. late Rawls, or which formulation of Kant actually applies.

Why it works: Judges vote for the team that understands the framework, not the team that name-drops it.

Judge Feedback

Paste any speech. Get paragraph-level feedback on structure, warrant quality, time allocation, and strategic gaps.

Why it works: The fastest way to improve is tight feedback loops. Go from feedback 4x a year to feedback every single practice session.

Strategic Mapper

See how arguments interact, where the real clash lives, and which points the judge will weigh. Think in systems, not bullets.

Why it works: The jump from novice to varsity is seeing the strategic landscape, not just having good individual arguments.

Argument Market

Submit a claim. The engine generates competing arguments, scores them on evidence and logic, then runs adversarial cycles. Bad arguments lose weight. Good ones rise.

Why it works: A prediction market for ideas. Run 3–5 cycles and watch it converge on which side actually holds up.
