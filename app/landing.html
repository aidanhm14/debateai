Tools

What you get

01 Case Generator

Enter a motion and side. Get a full case: framework, 3 arguments with warrant chains, compound impact, and A2 blocks for the top 5 opp responses.

Input: motion + side → Output: structured case

02 Round Simulator

Practice any speech type (PM, LO, MG, MO, LOR, PMR) against an AI that reads your arguments and responds with real counterarguments.

Input: your speech → Output: opponent response + judge notes

03 Philosophy Engine

50+ thinkers with debate-ready depth. Rawls (early vs. late), Kant (all 3 formulations), Foucault (archaeology vs. genealogy). Not Wikipedia summaries.

Input: thinker or concept → Output: debate-applicable breakdown

04 Judge Feedback

Paste a speech. Get line-by-line feedback on where warrants are thin, time allocation is off, or you dropped a key argument.

Input: speech text → Output: ballot-style critique

05 Strategic Mapper

See which arguments interact, where the real clash is, what opp will collapse on, and which points the judge weighs heaviest.

Input: motion → Output: argument interaction map

06 Argument Market

Submit a claim. Engine generates arguments for and against, scores them, then runs adversarial cycles. Weak arguments die, strong ones survive.

Input: a claim → Output: ranked, stress-tested arguments
How to use it

Three steps

1

Pick a tool

Open the app. Tap any of the six tabs. Each one works independently.

2

Give it input

Enter a motion, paste a speech, type a claim. Most results come back in under 30 seconds.

3

Iterate

Revise, ask follow-ups, copy to clipboard, or jump to another tool with the same motion.

Pricing

Why there's a token system

Every generation costs real money. The AI models that power this (Claude by Anthropic) charge per request. Instead of a flat monthly fee, you pay for what you use.

5 cases a month? Pay for 5. 50 cases? Pay for 50. This keeps the app sustainable and means we never have to downgrade to a cheaper, worse model.

During beta, everything is free. Every tool, unlimited usage, no credit card. We're testing with competitive debaters before launching paid plans.
