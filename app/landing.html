What's Inside

Six tools. One engine.

Case Generator

Enter a motion and side. Get framework, three arguments with warrant chains, compound impact, and A2 blocks for the top five opposition responses.

Round Simulator

Practice any speech type against an AI that reads your arguments and responds with real counterarguments. Timed like an actual round.

Philosophy Engine

50+ thinkers with debate-ready depth. Early vs. late Rawls, all three Kant formulations, Foucault's periods. Not Wikipedia summaries.

Judge Feedback

Paste a speech. Get line-by-line feedback on warrants, time allocation, dropped arguments, and what a judge would write on the ballot.

Strategic Mapper

See which arguments interact, where the real clash is, what opposition will collapse on, and which points the judge weighs heaviest.

Argument Market

Submit a claim. Engine generates competing arguments, scores them on evidence and logic, runs adversarial cycles. Weak arguments die.

Why I Built This

I started debating in college and immediately realized something — there's basically nothing out there built for competitive debaters. You're expected to construct cases, anticipate opposing arguments, understand frameworks and burdens, and do it all under insane time pressure. And the tools available? Google Docs and your own memory.

That's what bugged me. Debate is one of the most intellectually demanding activities you can do, and there wasn't a single piece of software designed to actually help you get better at it. Not one app that understood what a contention structure looks like, or how weighing works, or what it means to clash with an argument instead of just talking past it.

So I built one. Devil's Advocate exists because debaters deserve a tool that actually speaks their language. The case generator knows what frameworks are. The battle mode doesn't just sit there — it pushes back. The feedback engine tells you what arguments you missed, not just where you put a comma wrong.

But honestly, the bigger reason this exists is because debate changed how I think. It taught me how to break down any argument, see both sides of things most people only look at from one angle, and stay clear-headed when the pressure's on. Those skills show up everywhere — in careers, in conversations, in life. I wanted to build something that helps more people get there faster.
