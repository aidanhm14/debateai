Why It Works

The science is simple: reps win rounds

The best debaters aren't the smartest people in the room. They're the ones who've seen the most arguments, stress-tested the most cases, and practiced the most responses. Devil's Advocate compresses months of that exposure into hours.

Case Generator

Why it makes you better: You learn argument structure by seeing hundreds of well-built cases, not by reading a textbook. Every case the engine generates shows you a different way to frame a motion, construct a warrant, and pre-empt opposition. After generating 20 cases, you start pattern-matching instinctively — the same skill top debaters build over years of tournaments.

Philosophy Deep Dives

Why it makes you better: Judges vote for the team that understands their framework, not the team that name-drops it. Most debaters know Kant = "categorical imperative" and stop there. Devil's Advocate teaches you the three formulations, when each one applies, and exactly what an opponent will say to attack each one. The debater who knows which Rawls to cite (early vs. late) beats the debater who just says "veil of ignorance."

Argument Mapper

Why it makes you better: Winning debate rounds isn't about having the best individual argument. It's about seeing the strategic landscape — which arguments interact, where the clash actually is, and which points the judge will weigh heaviest. The mapper forces you to think in systems instead of bullets, which is the single biggest jump from novice to varsity-level strategic thinking.

Judge Feedback

Why it makes you better: The fastest way to improve at anything is tight feedback loops. Most debaters get feedback 4 times a year — at tournaments, from judges who give 2-minute oral critiques. Devil's Advocate gives you paragraph-level feedback on every speech you write, every time you practice. That's the difference between improving once a month and improving every day.

Round Simulator

Why it makes you better: You can't learn to box by hitting a bag. You need a sparring partner who hits back. The simulator gives you an AI opponent that responds to your actual arguments — not canned responses, but contextual counterarguments that force you to think on your feet. Practice your MG extension against an LO that actually attacks your case.

Practice Speeches

Why it makes you better: Debate is a performance art. Knowing the right argument isn't enough — you need to deliver it clearly under time pressure. The practice tool lets you write speeches against the clock and get immediate coaching on structure, word economy, and persuasive technique. The skills transfer directly: better practice speeches become better tournament speeches.

The Real Argument

Debate makes you dangerous everywhere

This tool makes you a better debater. But debate itself makes you a better everything.

The skills you build in parliamentary debate — constructing arguments under pressure, anticipating objections before they're raised, distilling complex ideas into persuasive language, thinking on your feet when the plan falls apart — these are the same skills that make people effective in law, business, policy, medicine, and every field where you need to convince smart people of hard things.

Every round you prep forces you to research a new topic deeply enough to argue it convincingly in 15 minutes. Over a season, you build working knowledge across economics, philosophy, international relations, technology, ethics, and public policy. You don't just learn what Rawls said — you learn it well enough to defend it against someone actively trying to tear it apart.

That's what Devil's Advocate accelerates. Not just tournament wins — the underlying cognitive skills that make debaters disproportionately successful after graduation. The pattern recognition. The ability to see both sides of any issue and articulate the stronger one. The confidence that comes from knowing you've already faced the hardest version of every objection.

The debaters who prep the most don't just win the most rounds. They become the most persuasive, knowledgeable, and adaptable thinkers in every room they walk into.
