The Engine

Six tools. One system.

Each one targets a specific bottleneck in debate prep. They work independently or together.

01

Case Generator

Enter any motion. Get a structured case with framework, interlocking arguments, warrant chains, compound impact, and pre-built A2 blocks for the five most likely opposition responses.

02

Round Simulator

Practice every speech type against an AI opponent that responds to your actual arguments — contextual counterarguments that force you to think on your feet.

03

Philosophy Engine

50+ philosophers with period-accurate depth. Know when to cite early vs. late Rawls, which Kant formulation applies, and what opponents will say to attack each framework.

04

Judge Feedback

Paste any speech. Get paragraph-level feedback on structure, warrant quality, time allocation, and strategic gaps — every practice session, not just tournament weekends.

05

Strategic Mapper

See how arguments interact, where the clash lives, and which points the judge will weigh. Think in systems instead of bullets — the jump from novice to varsity-level strategy.

06

Argument Market

Submit a claim. The engine generates competing arguments, scores them on evidence and logic, then runs adversarial cycles. Weak arguments lose weight. Strong ones rise.
