Our company philosophy

Every debate gathers public opinion.

The room votes on who moved them. That vote is a second verdict beside the AI ballot: it feeds speaker points, and it scores persuasion on people, not transcripts. One round moves one room. Thousands of rounds show what moves the public. The rooms themselves are mapped in the Debate Atlas.

The Floor · Prediction market

Bet on your words.

The Polymarket of debate. Every round is a market on one question: which side will the AI judge vote for? Take a position while they are still speaking. The ballot settles it seconds after the round ends, and the earlier you called it, the more it moves your Sharp Score.

The market model, applied to rounds instead of world events. The AI ballot is what settles every market.

FAQ

Common questions

Short answers. If something's not here, the last one routes to a real inbox.

What is this website?

A sparring partner with a clock and an opinion. Pick a motion and a side; the AI takes the other and argues.

You speak against the clock. It interrupts with POIs. At the end an AI judge writes the verdict: who won, why, a score per speech.

Fifteen formats. Fourteen languages. Free in beta.

Is it actually free?

Free in beta. One round without an account, 10 signed in with Google. No card.

The numbers on /pricing are the post-beta plan, public so you know what is coming. School or team access: email aidandavidhollinger@gmail.com.

Will it argue back, or just hallucinate arguments?

It argues, it does not fabricate. It presses soft claims, asks for warrants, and tells you when your logic collapses. It will never invent studies, cards, or tournament lore.

The harder problem is the opposite: it can sound too complete, missing the texture of a real round.

Isn't this just another AI debate tool?

Most hand you a wall of generic text. No clock, no format, no judge.

This runs a live round: a motion, a side, a clock that does not pause. It takes POIs in WSDC, reads at Policy speed, stays impromptu in APDA, then writes a real ballot.

It still says wrong things. Catching them is the drill.

Can I debate in another language?

Fourteen. English, Spanish, French, Portuguese, Mandarin, Arabic, Hindi, and more. Switch from the language pill and the AI argues, takes POIs, and writes the ballot in whichever you pick.

Never debated. Where do I start?

Quick Clash. Three-minute rounds, no rules to memorize. You take a side, the AI takes the other, the clock runs.

When that feels easy, /learn covers each format with rules, sample topics, and how judges score.

Do I need to sign up?

Not for the first round. After that, sign in with Google to save ballots across devices and switch on style learning, where the next opponent is biased against your habits.

Google only. No passwords, no marketing list.

Can my school get access for the whole team?

Yes. Team is $50/year after beta, built for clubs, classrooms, and shared program use. /schools has the longer pitch for coaches.

Running 50+ debaters? Email aidandavidhollinger@gmail.com.

For competitive debaters
Which formats?

Asian Parliamentary, WSDC, British Parliamentary, APDA, Public Forum, Lincoln-Douglas, Policy, Student Congress, MUN, plus Quick Clash for warmups.

Each runs its own prompt. WSDC and BP take POIs, LD goes framework-first, Policy spreads tagged cards, APDA stays impromptu.

Is using this to prep for tournaments cheating?

No.

What would be cheating: running it mid-round at a live competition, or passing AI-written case copy off as your own original prep.

How long is a round?

You pick. Quick Clash is three minutes. A full APDA round is about 45, BP about 60. Stop mid-round and it counts as practice.
