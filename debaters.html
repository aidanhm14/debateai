Format-accurate AI

The bot argues like someone who knows the format.

  • World Schools (WSDC)Matter / manner / method scoring, principled-plus-practical split, POIs after the first minute, international examples rather than US-default.
  • Asian Parliamentary (UADC-style)3v3 structure with the biased Reply speech, definitional debate when motions allow it, regional examples (Indian policy, ASEAN, Chinese regulation) that Asian judges expect.
  • British ParliamentaryFour-team dynamics, extension discipline for closing teams, whip-by-issues structure, "proud to propose." No fabricated citations since BP is impromptu.
  • Policy / CXTagged evidence ("Mearsheimer '14 continues..."), DA / CP / K / T off-case, 2NR collapse, stock issues debate.
  • Public ForumLay-accessible, evidence-heavy, explicit magnitude / probability / timeframe / scope weighing in Final Focus.
  • Lincoln-DouglasValue + criterion openings, framework debate, circuit kritiks and theory shells when the round warrants them.
  • APDATight cases and policy cases, PMR collapse, impromptu register, no fabricated "Smith 2022" citations.
  • Congress"Mr./Madam Speaker," real legislation by bill number, extemporaneous not scripted, "I urge an aye vote."
  • Model UNThird-person delegate voice, operative / preambulatory clauses, sovereignty language, citing real UN resolutions by number.
