How public figures use it

Three reps the pros actually run.

The people who look unflappable on stage rehearse for the question that would wreck them. This is that rehearsal, on demand, out loud.

01

Debate & panel prep

Simulate the opponent on stage — their strongest line of attack, their favorite pivot, the moment they'll try to rattle you. Drill the comeback until it's instinct. Works for political, business, and academic panels.

02

Media training

Hostile interviewer mode. AI probes the story you don't want to tell, loops on the answer you gave instead, and runs the "gotcha" setup you're going to see on 60 Minutes. Practice the bridge, the block, and the honest non-answer.

03

Keynote & town hall stress-test

Deliver your keynote. AI plays the tough Q&A that follows — the skeptical audience member, the reporter in the third row, the competitor's plant. Find the slide that triggers the hardest question and pre-answer it.

Who this is for

If your next appearance matters, rehearse it live.

  • Politicians + candidatesDebate prep, town hall Q&A, press conferences, oppo attack absorption. Drill the bridge and the pivot until they sound unrehearsed.
  • Executives + public CEOsEarnings calls, analyst Q&A, press interviews about a hard quarter. Practice the honest answer that doesn't tank the stock.
  • Podcasters + intervieweesLong-form interview prep. Drill the story, find where you sound rehearsed, find where you trail off. Make the anecdote land the fifth time as well as the first.
  • Panel speakers + conference keynotesStress-test the talk. Find the slide that invites the "yeah but" question nobody wants from the audience.
  • Activists + advocacy leadersHostile media prep, rally-speech rehearsal, opposition research drill. Practice under pressure so conviction doesn't read as defensiveness.
  • Journalists + reportersPrep the interview from the other side. Simulate the source's likely dodges and the follow-up that forces a real answer.
